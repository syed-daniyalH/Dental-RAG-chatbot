# Public Dental Support Chatbot

# Basic Questions and Answers Content

Document Name: `public_dental_chatbot_faq_v1.md`
Access Level: Public
Purpose: Basic chatbot Q&A content for dental claims, coding, denials, insurance terms, documents, and safe handoff responses.

---

## 1. What is a dental claim?

A dental claim is a request sent to an insurance carrier after a dental service is provided. It usually includes details such as the patient, dental office, provider, service date, procedure information, and billed amount.

The insurance carrier reviews the claim based on the patient's dental benefit plan. The claim may be paid, denied, rejected, delayed, or returned for more information.

For exact claim status, the user should contact their dental office or insurance carrier.

---

## 2. How does dental claim processing work?

Dental claim processing usually starts when the dental office submits a claim to the insurance carrier or clearinghouse. The claim is checked for required information, then reviewed under the patient's benefit plan.

The insurance carrier may approve payment, deny the claim, reject it because of missing information, or request additional documentation.

The final result is usually explained in an Explanation of Benefits, also called an EOB.

---

## 3. Why was my dental claim denied?

A dental claim may be denied for several general reasons, such as inactive coverage, missing documentation, waiting periods, frequency limits, annual maximum reached, service not covered, or plan exclusions.

The exact reason is usually shown on the Explanation of Benefits. For claim-specific details, the user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier.

---

## 4. Why was my dental claim rejected?

A claim may be rejected when it cannot be processed because required information is missing, invalid, or incomplete.

Common reasons include missing patient information, incorrect subscriber details, missing provider information, missing tooth number, missing tooth surface, missing attachment, or duplicate claim submission.

A rejected claim may often be corrected and resubmitted by the dental office or billing team.

---

## 5. What is the difference between a rejected claim and a denied claim?

A rejected claim usually means the claim could not be processed because something was missing or incorrect. It may need to be corrected and resubmitted.

A denied claim usually means the insurance carrier reviewed the claim but decided not to pay it based on the plan rules, coverage limits, exclusions, or missing requirements.

In simple terms: rejection is often a processing issue, while denial is usually a benefit or coverage decision.

---

## 6. What should I do if my dental claim is denied?

The user should review the Explanation of Benefits to find the denial reason. Then they should contact the dental office or insurance carrier to ask what can be done next.

Possible next steps may include submitting missing documentation, correcting claim information, requesting reconsideration, or filing an appeal if allowed by the plan.

The chatbot should not promise that a denial will be reversed.

---

## 7. What should I do if my dental claim is rejected?

If a claim is rejected, the user should ask the dental office or billing team what information is missing or incorrect.

Common corrections may include updating patient details, subscriber information, provider details, tooth number, tooth surface, procedure information, or required attachments.

After correction, the dental office may be able to resubmit the claim.

---

## 8. What is a dental procedure code?

A dental procedure code is a standardized code used to report a dental service on a claim. It helps the dental office and insurance carrier understand what service was performed.

The chatbot can explain dental coding concepts generally, but the correct code should be selected by the dental provider or billing professional based on the treatment performed and proper documentation.

---

## 9. Why do dental codes matter?

Dental codes matter because insurance carriers use them to review the service under the patient's benefit plan.

If the wrong code is submitted or required details are missing, the claim may be delayed, denied, rejected, or paid differently than expected.

The dental office or billing professional should review coding issues because the code must match the actual service performed.

---

## 10. Can you tell me which dental code should be used?

I can explain dental coding concepts in general, but I cannot choose the final procedure code for a specific treatment.

The correct dental code should be selected by the dentist, provider, or billing professional based on the service performed, clinical documentation, and applicable coding guidance.

For exact coding help, the user should contact the dental office or billing team.

---

## 11. Why is a tooth number required?

A tooth number may be required when the insurance carrier needs to know which tooth was treated.

This is common for tooth-specific services. If the tooth number is missing, the claim may be delayed, rejected, or returned for correction.

The chatbot should not guess the tooth number. The user should contact the dental office to confirm whether the claim needs the tooth number added or corrected.

---

## 12. Why is a tooth surface required?

A tooth surface describes the part of the tooth involved in the treatment.

Some procedures require surface information so the insurance carrier can review the claim properly. If the surface is missing, the claim may be delayed or rejected.

The dental office should provide the correct surface information based on the treatment record.

---

## 13. What does missing attachment mean?

Missing attachment means the insurance carrier may need supporting documentation before processing or paying the claim.

Examples of attachments may include X-rays, treatment notes, periodontal charts, images, narratives, or an Explanation of Benefits from another insurance carrier.

The user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier to confirm what attachment is required.

---

## 14. What documents may be needed for a dental claim?

Required documents can vary depending on the service, insurance carrier, and benefit plan.

Common examples may include X-rays, treatment notes, periodontal charting, intraoral images, narrative explanation, pre-authorization response, or an Explanation of Benefits from another carrier.

The public chatbot should not ask users to upload private records. The user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier for exact document requirements.

---

## 15. What is pre-authorization?

Pre-authorization is a review process where the insurance carrier reviews proposed treatment information before the service or before final claim processing.

It may also be called prior authorization, pre-treatment estimate, or predetermination.

Pre-authorization does not always guarantee payment. Final payment may still depend on eligibility, coverage, documentation, and plan rules.

---

## 16. Why is prior authorization required?

Some dental plans require prior authorization for certain services before payment can be considered.

If prior authorization was required but not completed, the claim may be delayed or denied.

The user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier to confirm whether prior authorization was required for the service.

---

## 17. What is an Explanation of Benefits?

An Explanation of Benefits, also called an EOB, is a document from the insurance carrier that explains how a claim was processed.

It may show the submitted charge, allowed amount, paid amount, patient responsibility, deductible, coinsurance, denial reason, or request for more information.

An EOB is not always a bill. It is an explanation of how the insurance carrier reviewed the claim.

---

## 18. Why did my insurance not pay the full amount?

Insurance may not pay the full amount because of deductible, coinsurance, copay, annual maximum, plan limitations, non-covered services, network status, waiting periods, or carrier allowed amount rules.

The exact reason should be reviewed on the Explanation of Benefits or confirmed with the insurance carrier.

---

## 19. What is a deductible?

A deductible is an amount the patient may need to pay before the insurance plan starts paying for certain covered services.

Not all services apply to a deductible. Some plans treat preventive, basic, and major services differently.

The user should contact the insurance carrier to confirm whether a deductible applies to their service.

---

## 20. What is coinsurance?

Coinsurance is the percentage of a covered service that the patient may be responsible for after insurance benefits are applied.

For example, if a plan covers part of a service, the remaining portion may be the patient's responsibility.

The chatbot should not calculate exact payment responsibility. The user should review the EOB or contact the insurance carrier.

---

## 21. What is a copay?

A copay is a fixed amount the patient may need to pay for certain services under a dental benefit plan.

Copay rules depend on the insurance plan and type of service.

The user should contact the insurance carrier or dental office to confirm whether a copay applies.

---

## 22. What is an annual maximum?

An annual maximum is the maximum amount a dental plan may pay for covered services during a benefit period.

If the annual maximum has already been reached, the insurance carrier may pay less or may not pay additional claims during that period.

The chatbot cannot check a user's remaining annual maximum. The user should contact the insurance carrier.

---

## 23. What is a waiting period?

A waiting period means the patient may need to be enrolled in the dental plan for a certain amount of time before some services are covered.

If the service was done before the waiting period ended, the claim may be denied.

Waiting periods vary by plan and service type. The user should confirm details with the insurance carrier.

---

## 24. What is a frequency limitation?

A frequency limitation is a plan rule that limits how often a service may be covered.

For example, some plans may only cover certain services once within a specific time period.

If the service was already used recently, the claim may be denied or paid differently.

---

## 25. What is coordination of benefits?

Coordination of Benefits, also called COB, applies when a patient has more than one insurance plan.

One plan may be primary and the other may be secondary. The secondary plan may need the primary plan's Explanation of Benefits before processing the claim.

If coordination information is missing, the claim may be delayed or denied.

---

## 26. What does service not covered mean?

Service not covered means the dental benefit plan does not provide benefits for that specific service, or the service is excluded under the plan rules.

A dentist may recommend a treatment even if the insurance plan does not cover it.

The user should review the EOB or contact the insurance carrier for exact coverage details.

---

## 27. What does duplicate claim mean?

A duplicate claim issue may happen when the insurance carrier believes the same service was already submitted or processed.

This can happen when a claim is submitted more than once or when a corrected claim is not clearly identified.

The user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier to confirm whether the claim was already received or processed.

---

## 28. Can this chatbot check my claim status?

No. This chatbot cannot check personal claim status or access private insurance records.

For exact claim status, the user should contact their dental office, billing team, or insurance carrier.

The chatbot can still explain general reasons why a dental claim may be delayed, rejected, or denied.

---

## 29. Can I provide my claim ID here?

No. Users should not provide claim IDs, subscriber IDs, insurance IDs, Social Security numbers, dates of birth, or private patient information in the public chatbot.

For claim-specific questions, the user should contact the dental office, billing team, or insurance carrier directly.

---

## 30. Can this chatbot access my insurance account?

No. This chatbot cannot access insurance accounts, patient records, benefit plans, claim files, or private records.

It can only provide general dental claims and insurance guidance.

For account-specific information, the user should contact their insurance carrier.

---

## 31. Why is my claim taking so long?

A dental claim may be delayed because of missing information, missing documentation, insurance review, coordination of benefits, eligibility issues, prior authorization requirements, or carrier processing time.

The chatbot cannot check the actual status of a claim.

The user should contact the dental office or insurance carrier for exact claim status.

---

## 32. What should I do if insurance asks for more information?

The user should contact the dental office or billing team and ask what information the insurance carrier requested.

The requested information may include X-rays, treatment notes, tooth number, tooth surface, provider details, or another insurance carrier's Explanation of Benefits.

The user should avoid sharing private documents in the public chatbot.

---

## 33. What should I do if my claim needs an appeal?

If a claim is denied and the user believes it should be reviewed again, they may ask the dental office or insurance carrier whether an appeal is available.

The user should review the EOB, collect supporting documents, and follow the insurance carrier's appeal process.

The chatbot can explain general appeal steps, but it cannot guarantee the result.

---

## 34. What should I ask my dental office if my claim fails?

The user can ask the dental office:

* Was the claim rejected or denied?
* What reason did the insurance carrier provide?
* Is any information missing?
* Were attachments submitted?
* Is tooth number or tooth surface required?
* Can the claim be corrected and resubmitted?
* Should I contact the insurance carrier?

---

## 35. What should I ask my insurance carrier if my claim fails?

The user can ask the insurance carrier:

* What is the exact reason for the denial or rejection?
* Was coverage active on the service date?
* Is the service covered under my plan?
* Is prior authorization required?
* Was any attachment missing?
* Does a waiting period or frequency limit apply?
* What are the next steps?

---

## 36. What does inactive coverage mean?

Inactive coverage means the insurance carrier may not show active benefits for the patient on the date of service.

This can happen because of plan termination, employment changes, missed premiums, or plan updates.

The user should contact the insurance carrier to confirm coverage status for the service date.

---

## 37. What does out-of-network mean?

Out-of-network means the dental provider may not have a contract with the insurance carrier or plan.

Claims may be processed differently for out-of-network providers, and patient responsibility may be higher.

The user should contact the insurance carrier or dental office to confirm network status.

---

## 38. What is patient responsibility?

Patient responsibility is the amount the patient may owe after insurance processes the claim.

It may include deductible, copay, coinsurance, non-covered services, amounts over plan limits, or services not paid by insurance.

The exact amount should be reviewed on the EOB or confirmed with the dental office or insurance carrier.

---

## 39. Can this chatbot tell me if a service is covered?

The chatbot can explain coverage concepts generally, but it cannot verify personal benefits or plan-specific coverage.

Coverage depends on the user's insurance plan, eligibility, limitations, exclusions, and carrier review.

The user should contact the insurance carrier to confirm whether a service is covered.

---

## 40. Final general disclaimer

This chatbot provides general dental claims, coding, and insurance guidance for educational support only.

It cannot access private records, check claim status, verify benefits, calculate exact patient responsibility, or make final claim decisions.

For exact information about a claim, benefit plan, or patient responsibility, users should contact their dental office, billing team, or insurance carrier.
